Dark Web
Monitoring

Stolen credentials, leaked data, and planned attacks are routinely traded on dark web marketplaces and underground forums. Our practice monitors these sources to provide early warning of threats targeting your organization — before they materialize as incidents.

Visibility Into the Underground

The dark web and criminal underground represent the supply chain of cybercrime. Stolen employee credentials, customer databases, intellectual property, and organizational reconnaissance are bought and sold on markets and forums that are invisible to conventional security monitoring.

Our dark web monitoring service provides continuous surveillance of these sources for indicators specific to your organization — exposed credentials, data mentions, brand impersonation, infrastructure references, and planned targeting. Early detection of these indicators enables proactive defensive action before the information is weaponized.

Capabilities

  • Credential exposure monitoring across dark web markets and paste sites
  • Employee email and password pair detection with breach source attribution
  • Customer and partner data exposure identification
  • Organizational mention and targeting intelligence from underground forums
  • Brand impersonation and phishing kit detection on dark markets
  • Threat actor campaign tracking relevant to your industry
  • Executive name and personal data monitoring
  • Actionable alert delivery with remediation recommendations
  • Monthly intelligence summary reports

Who We Serve

Organizations concerned about credential exposure from third-party breaches. Companies in industries frequently targeted by cybercriminal groups. Security teams seeking early warning of planned attacks. Enterprises managing large user populations with password reuse risk.

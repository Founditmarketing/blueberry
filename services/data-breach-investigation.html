Forensic Investigation

Data Breach
Investigation

Determining the full scope of a data breach — what was accessed, when, and by whom — requires rigorous forensic methodology. Our practice conducts defensible investigations that satisfy legal counsel, regulators, and insurance carriers.

Establishing the Facts

After a breach is contained, the critical question becomes: what data was compromised? The answer determines regulatory notification obligations, legal exposure, insurance coverage, and customer communication requirements. Our forensic investigations provide definitive answers supported by evidence that withstands legal scrutiny.

We conduct systematic data breach investigations that trace attacker activity from initial access through every system and data repository they touched. Our methodology produces comprehensive timelines, data exposure assessments, and forensic reports that serve as the foundation for legal, regulatory, and business decisions.

Investigation Methodology

  • Full-scope forensic analysis across endpoints, servers, and cloud infrastructure
  • Attacker timeline reconstruction — initial access through final exfiltration
  • Data exposure quantification — precisely which records were accessed or stolen
  • PII, PHI, and financial data classification of compromised records
  • Exfiltration pathway analysis — how data left the environment
  • Regulatory notification assessment across all applicable jurisdictions
  • Forensic report preparation for legal, regulatory, and insurance purposes
  • Evidence preservation with defensible chain of custody documentation
  • Remediation roadmap to address the vulnerabilities that enabled the breach

Who We Serve

Organizations that have experienced a confirmed or suspected data breach. Breach counsel attorneys requiring privileged forensic investigation. Insurance carriers investigating the scope of policyholder claims. Compliance officers managing regulatory notification obligations.

Investigation Scope

  • Endpoint & Server Forensics
  • Cloud Environment Analysis
  • Data Exposure Assessment
  • Timeline Reconstruction
  • Regulatory Guidance
  • Legal-Ready Documentation

Defense Compliance

NIST 800-171
Compliance

NIST Special Publication 800-171 defines the security requirements for protecting Controlled Unclassified Information in non-federal systems. Our practice helps defense contractors assess, implement, and maintain compliance across all 110 security controls.

Protecting Controlled Unclassified Information

NIST 800-171 compliance is not optional for organizations handling CUI within the defense supply chain. The 110 security requirements across 14 control families establish the baseline security posture required by DFARS clause 252.204-7012 — and form the foundation for CMMC Level 2 certification.

Our team conducts thorough gap assessments against every control requirement, develops prioritized remediation plans, and provides implementation guidance that addresses both the technical and procedural dimensions of compliance. Every engagement produces documentation that satisfies both DFARS reporting requirements and CMMC assessment preparation.

Capabilities

  • Full 110-control gap assessment and scoring
  • System Security Plan (SSP) development and documentation
  • Plan of Action & Milestones (POA&M) management
  • CUI identification, scoping, and data flow mapping
  • Access control implementation and identity management
  • Audit logging and monitoring configuration
  • Encryption and data protection controls
  • Incident response procedure development
  • Continuous monitoring program implementation

Who We Serve

Defense contractors subject to DFARS 252.204-7012. Organizations preparing for CMMC Level 2 assessment. Subcontractors receiving CUI from prime contractors. Companies seeking to self-assess their NIST 800-171 compliance score.

Control Families

  • Access Control
  • Audit & Accountability
  • Configuration Management
  • Identification & Authentication
  • Incident Response
  • Media Protection
  • System & Communications Protection
